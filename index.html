EconoMed: Medicare Cost Calculator

Value-based care by the numbers.

When healthcare providers are caring for a patient, the costs of labs, procedures, and medications can add up. Get an idea for how much your care plan will cost to Medicare with the EconoMed tool. Tap a cost type below to get started.
Medicare Part B covers the cost to patients receiving emergency department services, observation services, outpatient surgery, lab tests, or X-rays, as long as they have not been admitted to the hospital as an inpatient. Literature has demonstrated that some of these interventions are of low value (=quality/cost) and these will be highlighted (tap/hover for more info).

Medicare Part A covers the cost to patients admitted to the hospital up until the day before they are discharged. Treatment is lumped, with price set by the primary diagnosis necessitating admission, along with the presence of complicating conditions (CC) which can be considered major (MCC).

Medicare Part D covers the cost of self-administered medications for those who have added this additional coverage after having enrolled in Medicare Part A+B. Costs are shown per dosage unit and are thus most useful for understanding the relative price-tags of various treatments. For example, comparing between drugs, or comparing generic vs brand name drugs.
